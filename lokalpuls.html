Lokalpuls

Local social platform for community question asking

Role: UX/UI Designer — Duration: 3 months — Team: 3 Designers, 1 Dev, 1 PM

1. Problem

In smaller communities, there’s often a lack of digital platforms where people can easily ask questions and get local answers. Existing solutions like Facebook groups or WhatsApp chats are unstructured and often overwhelming.

2. Goal

Create a simple and accessible platform for locals to ask questions and receive reliable, contextual answers from nearby users and institutions.

3. Research & Insights

  • We interviewed 15 local residents aged 20–65
  • Identified pain points: noise, lack of trust, unclear responses
  • Benchmarked Reddit, Nebenan.de, and Facebook groups

4. Ideation

We explored user flows, information architecture, and early wireframes. Accessibility and clarity were our primary concerns.

5. Design & UX Solutions

The UI is designed to feel calm and approachable, with a focus on location, categories, and response clarity. The ask-flow is reduced to 3 intuitive steps.

6. Prototyping & Testing

Clickable prototypes were tested with 8 users from the initial interviews. Improvements were made to question categorization and notification settings.

View prototype

7. Result & Impact

The platform was launched in 2 small towns and received over 500 user-generated questions in the first month. Local institutions joined as verified responders.

8. Reflection

I learned how crucial community trust and onboarding are in local apps. If I could go back, I’d involve institutional partners earlier in the design process.
